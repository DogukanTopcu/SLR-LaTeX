\section{Threats to Validity}
This review has several threats to validity that should be stated explicitly.

\textbf{Internal validity.} Although the review follows a PRISMA-guided workflow, study identification, screening, and extraction still involve researcher judgment. Borderline papers may have been interpreted differently under another reading of the inclusion criteria, especially in cases where the SLM component or the empirical contribution was not equally central throughout the paper. The representative PDF inspection used to deepen the narrative also adds a second judgment layer, because not every included study was read in the same depth. To reduce this risk, the manuscript keeps the synthesis tied to the predefined research questions and uses the same extraction matrix throughout.

\textbf{Construct validity.} Some of the core constructs in this review are not standardized across the literature. In particular, ``SLM'', ``hybrid architecture'', ``multi-agent system'', and ``efficiency'' are defined differently from paper to paper. A single hybrid label may refer to token-level verification, retrieval-grounded generation, planner-based decomposition, or multimodal fusion. Likewise, efficiency may mean latency, FLOPs, API cost, bandwidth reduction, or energy use. This threatens direct comparability and means that some studies grouped under the same high-level category differ substantially in mechanism.

\textbf{Conclusion validity.} The strongest limitation for cross-study inference is the uneven and inconsistent reporting of evaluation outcomes. Accuracy is reported in most papers, but latency, cost, and energy are available only in a minority, often with different units, hardware assumptions, or deployment settings. For that reason, the review can identify recurring patterns, but it cannot support a formal meta-analysis or strong quantitative claims about the size of efficiency gains across the whole corpus. This threat is reinforced by publication and reporting bias, since positive hybrid results are more likely to be published and summarized than negative or inconclusive findings.

\textbf{External validity.} The corpus is based on a Web of Science-centered search and a bounded review window. This improves transparency and reproducibility, but it may miss relevant studies from other databases, preprint platforms, or fast-moving venues. The field also evolves quickly, and the current corpus is heavily concentrated in 2025 and in a limited set of application domains. Therefore, the conclusions should be generalized cautiously beyond the covered time period, tasks, and deployment settings.
