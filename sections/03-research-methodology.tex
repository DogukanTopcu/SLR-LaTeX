\section{Research Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

\subsection{Research Questions}
\label{sec:research_questions}

To systematically evaluate the technical and operational trade-offs between monolithic, hybrid, and multi-agent architectures, the review protocol is structured around the following four core research questions (RQs):

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{RQ1 (Performance):} How does the performance (accuracy/efficacy) of multi-agent SLMs or hybrid architectures (LLM + SLM) compare with that of single monolithic LLMs in specific reasoning and classification tasks?
    
    \item \textbf{RQ2 (Efficiency):} What is the impact of Hybrid and Multi-agent architectures on computational efficiency, specifically regarding inference cost, latency, and energy consumption, compared to Monolithic models?
    
    \item \textbf{RQ3 (Orchestration):} What orchestration and routing mechanisms are employed to manage the division of labor between large and small models in hybrid systems?
    
    \item \textbf{RQ4 (Domain Suitability):} In which specific domains (e.g., Healthcare, Coding, Mathematics) do SLMs achieve performance comparable to LLMs, and where do they fail (e.g., long-context reasoning)?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Information Sources and Search Strategy}
\label{sec:search_strategy}

To capture a comprehensive and high-quality corpus of contemporary language model architectures, the literature search was conducted utilizing the Web of Science (WoS) Core Collection. Utilizing WoS as the primary search engine allowed for the retrieval of indexed peer-reviewed journal articles, conference proceedings, and highly cited preprints within a single dataset.

The search strategy was systematically developed by extracting the core concepts from the defined research questions and refining the terminology through initial pilot searches. This iterative process helped identify common synonyms and alternate phrasing used in the literature (e.g., ``Compact Model'' for SLMs, or ``Agentic'' for multi-agent systems). To guarantee that every retrieved study examined the intersection of model size, architectural design, and system evaluation, the final query was structured into four mandatory components. The following Boolean query was applied to the title, abstract, and keyword fields:

\begin{quote}
( "Large Language Model*" OR "LLM" OR "Generative AI" OR "Foundation Model*" ) AND 
( "Small Language Model*" OR "SLM" OR "Compact Model*" OR "Lightweight Model*" ) AND 
( "Multi-agent" OR "Hybrid" OR "Orchestration" OR "Routing" OR "Ensemble" OR "Agentic" ) AND 
( "Performance" OR "Accuracy" OR "Efficiency" OR "Latency" OR "Cost" OR "Benchmark" )
\end{quote}

\subsection{Study Selection Process}
\label{sec:study_selection}

The study selection process followed PRISMA 2020 guidelines \cite{page2021prisma}, as shown in Figure \ref{fig:prisma_flowchart}. The Web of Science Core Collection search yielded 116 records. During Identification and Screening, 36 records were excluded (non-technical surveys, insufficient validation, unrelated domains), leaving 80 for full-text review. In the Assessment phase, 26 additional records were excluded: 18 for model size/architecture mismatch, 5 for out-of-scope topics, and 3 for lacking empirical data. Inclusion and exclusion decisions were made via majority vote with team consensus, resulting in a final corpus of 54 primary studies.

Extracted fields included SLM and LLM models, architecture type, orchestration mechanisms, evaluation metrics, baseline models, and performance indicators (accuracy, latency, cost, energy). Following data extraction, a narrative synthesis approach was utilized. Because the included studies operate across diverse domains (e.g., telecommunications, healthcare, software engineering) and report varying metrics, a purely statistical meta-analysis was not feasible. Instead, studies were grouped by their architectural taxonomy, allowing for a comparative analysis of how different orchestration methods and domain constraints impact the performance and efficiency trade-offs between large and small models.
