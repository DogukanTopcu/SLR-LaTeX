\section{Related Works}

Prior review literature on SLMs and SLM--LLM collaboration includes four highly relevant works. The closest is a recent PRISMA 2020-guided SLR with a reproducible multi-database selection protocol, but it adopts a broader scope on SLMs in general and offers limited coverage of the 2025--2026 wave of hybrid and agent-based systems~\cite{slmreview2025prisma}.

The remaining three are narrative surveys with substantial thematic overlap. They address SLM definitions, training techniques, collaboration patterns, trustworthiness, and edge-oriented deployment concerns, but they do not provide a formal PRISMA-guided study selection and extraction framework~\cite{wang2024slmsurvey,chen2025collabmech,wang2025collabperf}.

The gap is therefore not attention to SLMs, but analytical emphasis and evidential grounding. Existing surveys describe models and mechanisms, yet rarely align empirical evidence across domains or compare operational metrics systematically. In contrast, our study provides a PRISMA-guided, engineering-oriented synthesis that treats domain suitability as a first-class axis and emphasizes quantitative indicators such as accuracy, latency, cost, and energy.
