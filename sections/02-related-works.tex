\section{Related Works}

Prior review literature on Small Language Models (SLMs) and SLM--LLM collaboration includes four highly relevant works. The closest study is a recent PRISMA 2020-guided SLR that uses a reproducible multi-database selection protocol, but it adopts a broader topical scope on SLMs in general and provides limited coverage of the 2025--2026 wave of hybrid and agent-based systems~\cite{slmreview2025prisma}.

The remaining three works are narrative surveys with substantial thematic overlap. They cover topics such as SLM definitions, training techniques, collaboration patterns, trustworthiness, and edge-oriented deployment concerns, but do not provide a formal PRISMA-guided study selection and extraction framework~\cite{wang2024slmsurvey,chen2025collabmech,wang2025collabperf}.

Thus, the gap is not a lack of attention to SLMs or collaboration, but a difference in analytical emphasis and evidential grounding. Existing surveys describe models and mechanisms, yet rarely align empirical evidence across domains or report comparable operational metrics. In contrast, our study provides a PRISMA-guided, engineering-oriented synthesis that treats domain suitability as a first-class axis, maps research questions to structured extraction fields, and emphasizes quantitative indicators such as accuracy, latency, cost, and energy.
